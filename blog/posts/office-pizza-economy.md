The Office Pizza has no NPC source. You can't earn it from any character on campus. It just shows up in your inventory sometimes. Nobody knows the exact conditions, and that's the point.

## The social ones

Some of the 17 odd items aren't about what they do. They're about what happens between people because of them. A student gets a Hot Potato that passes itself to someone nearby, and suddenly two people who've never spoken are laughing about a potato. That's the item working exactly right.

### 🥔 Hot Potato

**Rarity:** Common · **Source:** Night Owl (10%)

*"It is extremely hot. Why is it so hot? Just a potato."*

The Hot Potato passes itself to a nearby player every 30 seconds. It keeps going, person to person, creating a little chain across campus. When it finally expires, there's a small cosmetic pop.

![The Hot Potato, passes to a nearby friend every 30 seconds](images/odd-items-hot-potato.png)

What I'm hoping happens: students start noticing each other. They cluster together. They type each other's names in chat for the first time because they want to know who just got the potato. Then it's over, and they go back to coding, but now they know someone new.

### 🧦 Unmatched Sock

**Rarity:** Common · **Source:** Byte (20%)

*"Somehow warm. Where is the other one?"*

The Unmatched Sock can be traded for any item of equal or lesser rarity. One mechanic, but it should turn the whole item system into a barter economy. "I'll trade you two socks for a Consulting Duck." "Three socks for a Glitter Bomb?"

I didn't design an economy. I designed a sock. But once people have something tradeable, they start thinking about value, and more importantly, they start talking to each other to make deals.

### 🍕 Office Pizza

**Rarity:** Uncommon · **Source:** No NPC source

*"Two hours old. Cold. Still kind of good?"*

The Office Pizza grants +2 slices to your Pizza Book Club membership, but only if you've joined the club. This connects different parts of campus: the item system talks to the club system, and students who explore multiple features get a small reward for it.

But the real thing is the mystery. Where does it come from? I'm betting students will form theories: "It appears after your third consecutive hour studying." "I think it shows up at midnight." "It only spawns when you're the only one in a room." None of these will be confirmed. The wondering keeps people exploring.

### 📜 Haunted Syllabus

**Rarity:** Uncommon · **Source:** Prof Ada (30%)

*"47 pages. All due dates were yesterday. You don't know how you got this."*

Every student who reads that flavor text will laugh, because they've all felt that exact moment of academic overwhelm. The Haunted Syllabus turns a universal student experience into a collectible. It's a work culture roast for school: "we've all been there."

### 📋 Vague Feedback

**Rarity:** Common · **Source:** Prof Ada (35%)

*"'Interesting work.' That is the entirety of the feedback."*

When you hold Vague Feedback, nearby players periodically say "hmm" in ambient chat. It's the subtlest item in the whole set. Most people won't notice at first. When they figure out where the "hmm" is coming from, that moment of realization is the whole joke.

If you've ever gotten a performance review that said nothing, this one's for you.

### 🪪 Expired Hall Pass and others

The **Expired Hall Pass** gives you a 50/50 coin flip on whether you skip an event. The **Heavy Backpack** adds a cosmetic lean to your character, no gameplay effect, just a vibe. **Group Chat (Muted)** silences notification pings for 5 minutes, and its description (*"247 unread messages. It is fine. Everything is fine."*) is a direct roast of every work chat you've ever muted and pretended was fine.

## The accidental economy

I didn't set out to build an economy. I set out to build conversation starters, little moments of "hey, look at this" that give students a reason to talk to each other.

But when you give people items with different rarities and a way to trade them, they start connecting on their own. They learn each other's names because they want to ask "do you have a Sock?" They explore campus because they heard Rinley drops something good. They come back because they're curious what they'll find.

These aren't live yet, but the systems are built and the items are ready. I'm excited to see what kind of economy a sock creates.
