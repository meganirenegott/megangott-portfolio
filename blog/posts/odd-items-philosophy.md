A lot of our students are here because they got tired of waiting for someone else to build the thing they wanted to build. They're learning to code so they can create their own work, on their own terms, instead of depending on employers who may not care about them. Along the way they're finding collaborators, getting honest feedback, and taking risks in a place that actually supports that.

So when I designed 17 odd items for the campus inventory, I wasn't thinking about gamification. I was thinking about all the worst parts of a corporate job and how funny they are when you shrink them down and hand them to someone as a gift.

## Work culture, but make it absurd

Most of these items are roasts. Vague Feedback makes everyone nearby periodically say "hmm," which is exactly how most performance reviews feel. The Fake Meeting gives you 3 minutes of item immunity, because the real version of that meeting didn't need to happen either. The Almost Done Project™ has a cosmetic "Scope Creep" counter that just keeps going up. The Unsolicited Opinion auto-sends helpful-ish advice in chat, whether anyone asked or not.

If you've worked in an office, you've lived these. If you haven't yet, now you know what you're not missing.

## Why they work as social lubricant

The real function of these items isn't the joke. It's what happens after the joke.

The Hot Potato auto-passes to a nearby friend every 30 seconds, which means you suddenly have a reason to be near someone. The Glitter Bomb covers a room in sparkles, and now everyone in that room has a shared experience. The Consulting Duck walks you through rubber duck debugging, and someone watching you talk to a pixel duck is going to ask what you're doing.

Every item is a conversation starter disguised as a toy. In a learning environment where people are still figuring out who to talk to and who to trust, a silly object that gives you something to laugh about together is doing more work than it looks like.

## The rarity system

Items come in five tiers:

- **Common**: everyday workplace humor (Vague Feedback, Unmatched Sock, Hot Potato, Box of Snakes)
- **Uncommon**: the heart of the collection (Glitter Bomb, Consulting Duck, Haunted Syllabus, Office Pizza, Roomba with Googly Eyes)
- **Rare**: a little harder to find (Fake Meeting, Expired Hall Pass)
- **Epic**: one item lives here (Almost Done Project™), relatable to a fault
- **Abhorrent**: items so wonderfully weird they needed their own tier (Cursed USB Drive, Unsolicited Opinion)

"Abhorrent" is a joke rarity. It's the tier for things that are too good to be called bad.

## How students find them

Items are gifted by NPCs across campus, each with their own personality and drop pool. Baba Yaga might hand you a Cursed USB Drive. Rinley might surprise you with a Roomba that has googly eyes. Prof Ada has a fondness for the Haunted Syllabus.

Students end up exploring the campus without realizing it. They talk to NPCs they haven't met yet. They visit parts of the map they wouldn't otherwise. The item system is quietly an exploration incentive disguised as a collection of jokes about corporate life.

## What I'm hoping happens

These aren't live yet, but they're close. I keep thinking about someone getting a Vague Feedback and immediately showing the person next to them. Or two students trading Unmatched Socks and realizing they actually like working together. Or someone Glitter Bombing the main lecture hall and everyone in it having a story to tell.

The items are jokes about the working world, but the connections they create are real. That's kind of the whole point of this place.
